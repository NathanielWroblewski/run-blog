Friday, February 10th, 2015 New Braunfels, TX

Tonight was the first night I've run without music, and the run seemed a lot harder. I felt shin splints about 1.5-2 miles in, which really slowed me down. This is the first time I've felt pain while running, and I can't help but wonder if these were my first shin splints or if it was because I didn't have music to distract me.

Throughout the run, my pace felt slowed because of the pain, but looking at the data afterwards, it wasn't that bad of a run. I stayed within the 8-9 min/mi range, which is where I wanted to be.

PACE

The Garmin is nice in that it doesn't bounce around like my phone but also because it tracks a wealth of metrics that my phone simply can't. The one I'm focusing on improving first is going to be cadence, because its the most well-studied. Starting with a 5% increase in cadence should increase my pace significantly. To do so, I'm going shopping for a lightweight, digital metronome that I can wear while I run. Over time, I'm hoping I'll adopt the metronome's tempo as my own.

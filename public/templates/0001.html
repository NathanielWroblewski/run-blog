ROUGH START

Friday, February 6th, 2015 New Braunfels, TX

I'm a big guy. At 6'1" and 220lbs., I probably shouldn't even be running in the first place. But I promised a friend of mine that I'd run a marathon with her, and I'm a man of my word. Her goal is to qualify for Boston; mine is to keep up. That means running 26.2 miles in 3 hours and 30 minutes, flat 8 minute miles, which right now, is unfathomable. This is a blog about tracking runs, making sacrifices, and getting good in a short amount of time.

You join me in a pretty sorry state. I'm slow, I've got bum knees, and every time I run, my teeth hurt for some reason. After tracking my first run, I discovered that I was pulling down 11 minute miles. That's not even running by most standards. That's jogging.

As a runner, you'll read a lot of books that talk about perfect form, but despite my best efforts, I seem to be embracing the imperfect form, something akin to an injured buffalo. While I've only just started this running thing, I'm not off to a very good start, but despite my hideous form and my body jogging when I really mean it to run, I'm not about to give up just yet.

Mostly because I really want to do this. I want to see if I can do this. I'm also hoping that by running, I'll get out more, see more of the world, and get a new perspective on things.

Because I don't have much time before the race in December, I'm going to try several different approaches to running, and unlike other sites out there, I'll be measuring the differences and posting the results. Hopefully, its these experiences that you can learn from.

If you've got some time between now and December, join me on my adventure as I run by the numbers.

Friday, February 6th, 2015 New Braunfels, TX

I'm a big guy. At 6'1" and 220lbs., I probably shouldn't even be running in the first place. But I promised a friend of mine that I'd run a marathon with her, and I'm a man of my word. Her goal is to qualify for Boston; mine is to keep up. That means running 26.2 miles in 3 hours and 30 minutes, flat 8 minute miles, which right now, is unfathomable.

PACE

ELEVATION

You're joining me right now in a pretty sorry state. I'm slow, I've got bum knees, and every time I run, my teeth hurt for some reason. After tracking my first run, I discovered that I was pulling down 11 minute miles. That's not even running by most standards. That's jogging. But despite terrible form and a body that jogs when I really mean for it to run, I'm not giving up just yet.

CURRENT

Mostly because I really want to do this. I want to see if I can do this. I'm hoping that with running, I'll get out more, see more of the world, and get a new perspective on things.

Unfortunately, there's not a lot of time between now and the marathon in December, so I'll need to try a lot of different things to see what works. Aside from being an attempt to keep me motivated, this blog is a chance for me to organize those experiences, reflect on my runs, and identify what works.

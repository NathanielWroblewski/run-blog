ROUGH START

Friday, February 6th, 2015 New Braunfels, TX

I'm a big guy. At 6'1" and 220lbs., I probably shouldn't even be running in the first place. But I promised a friend of mine that I'd run a marathon with her, and I'm a man of my word. Her goal is to qualify for Boston; mine is to keep up. That means running 26.2 miles in 3 hours and 30 minutes, flat 8 minute miles, which right now, is unfathomable. This is a blog about tracking runs, making sacrifices, and getting good in a short amount of time.

You join me in a pretty sorry state. I'm slow, I've got bum knees, and every time I run, my teeth hurt for some reason. After tracking my first run, I discovered that I was pulling down 11 minute miles. That's not even running by most standards. That's jogging. But despite my terrible form and my body jogging when I really mean for it to run, I'm not about to give up just yet.

Mostly because I really want to do this. I want to see if I can do this. I'm also hoping that by running, I'll get out more, see more of the world, and get a new perspective on things.

Because I don't have much time before the race in December, I'm going to be trying a lot of different things, to get as good as I can in as short a time as possible. I promise to take measurements and post the results, so if you've got some time between now and December, join me on my adventure, and we can learn together as I run by the numbers.
